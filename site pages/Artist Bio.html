Hello I am Jacob Isaacs,

My history as an artist started at an extremely young age. The time that I could pick up a pen or paintbrush, was the day I became an artist.

My favorite class in elementary school was taught by a teacher that also happened to specialize in art therapy. As a tiny sprout I was taught that art could be used to express my emotions and I could use it as a tool to find and define myself as a person.

After taking the class my fondest memories were with a friend named Austin Williams.

Austin and I would draw together on the same piece of paper, and we both found a deep appreciation for aquatic life, so we mainly drew sharks and weird deep sea creatrues.

My best friend Austin eventually had to move to washington state, because that's where his dad found a job. It was then that my parents signed me up for several art classes outside of school.

I took art classes at the Visual Arts Center of Richmond.

The classes that I took were animation, photography, creative writing, sculpting, painting and drawing.

All through out middle school I continued to learn and experiment with drawing until I found myself old enough to enter High School.

Once I got into High School my first choice in electives was of course Fine Art.

When I took the first Fine Art course I found myself loving it so much that I ended up going onto the Highest level of Fine Art that my High School offered, Fine Art III.

After High School I somehow ended up going to school for Engineering.

I now have a Bachelors of Science in Mathematics with a concentration in Operations Research.

Eventhough I went through intense Mathematical Study I never gave up on Art, my one true passion.

While I was in school I decided to experiment with ways to make my art more than just a hobby, but a way of earning a living.

I showcased my work in several different Galleries and ended up losing quite a bit of money.

I then experimented at several different venues with selling different products with my paintings.

I was more successful selling trinkets than actual pieces, which lead me towards the idea of merchandising my work.

After several different experiments I believe that I have finally come across a solution.
